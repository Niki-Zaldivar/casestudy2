Lifeline Animal Project

An endeavor to match pets with the perfect home,
by using gamification to delight users, and open their hearts to the unexpected in
one convenient mobile application.

Summary


LifeLine Animal Project is an Atlanta-based animal shelter
leading the way to make Atlanta a no-kill zone for animals.
Their goal is to take animals off the street and put them into
loving homes be it fosters or adopters.

My Role

Ux design team member

With my unique skills with Communication, User Research and User Insight,
I helped guide my team to discovering clear problems and presenting
a cohesive solution through dynamic and delightful design.

Project Overview

The Scope of the Project

A Course of Action

We established the scope of work through our initial research
into the needs of the organization by interviewing a key stakeholder and developing
a plan of action based on the current style of the website and the lack of a usable mobile
application. We broke down our goals over a period of three weeks.

  • First, we created a survey and launched it over social media
    and personal contacts as well as individuals associated
    with Lifeline.
  • Next, we developed an interview plan and sought out individuals
    that met the requirements of our protopersona
  • With thorough analysis, we were able to define our goals and
    prioritze the most important features of our application
  • Through paper sketches and lo fidelity wireframing, we were able
    to design a cohesive style and consistent UI elements
  • The testing phase proved to be fruitful and left us feeling
    accomplished in meeting the needs of both the users and the
    stakeholders
  • Finally, we presented our findings to the stakeholder for review

App Feature Highlights & Mockups

The first feature neccessary in this partnered app redesign was a simplified login process and a streamlined homepage navigation. Lifeline Animal Project currently has different login portals for each branch of the organization, making it difficult for the user to participate in community outreach in multiple areas without unneccessary effort. The homescreen was transformed to feature a weekly pet that is ready to be adopted. It was very important to the stakeholders at Lifeline that those animals that may be hard to place, are featured above all else. So older dogs and cats, those with manageable health concerns, and breeds deemed aggressive without due cause. These animals are harder to place, and so they are more in need of special attention.

Pet Match Feature

The most important, and our most proud highlight, is the development of our swiping pet match feature and the partnered quiz. Our stakeholders expressed a desire to obtain an easier way to match and connect potential adoptive pet owners with new furry friends while considering Their lifestyles and desires in a new companion. Through gamification and swiping, we were able to synthesize a system that delights the user and populates real and useful results.

My Process

We conducted our interviews first; we found people who we knew that had adopted, fostered, or volunteered with a shelter. As a bonus, more than one of them had affiliated with Lifeline themselves! Our Stakeholder highlighted the importance of all three branches and mentioned a need for streamlined matchmaking, scheduling, and helping users feel confident about homing a large dog without prejudice. After 8 user interviews and our stakeholder interview, we collected and analyzed our data. We found that our users were interested in matchmaking and more contact with shelter staff in the process of adoption and fostering. We also found that people generally didn't know enough about fostering, and therefore, did not participate in what the Stakeholder described as “a crucial part of the animal caregiving process.”

Insights

Personal Realizations

The process of understanding the broad spectrum of users who may encounter our app
led us in a multitude of directions. It became clear that we needed our app to focus
on the 3 main branches of participation offered by Lifeline Animal Project;
Adopt, Foster, and Volunteer.

While we expected that most of our users would be seeking the love of a pet, we also
understood that the individual may be seeking to help the organization themselves in
their capacity to aid the community. With this, we sought to make the process of matching
an animal with the right home a streamlined process as well as a fun and delightful experience.
Our team had varrying degrees of love for our furry friends, but at the end of the day,
none were lacking in the compassion needed to get the job done.

Story Boarding & User Flow

With three distinct branches (Adopt, Foster, and Volunteer) We developed a story for each, but we focused on the Foster story because it is easily nested between adopting and volunteering. The story we developed follows a young woman and her roommate in an endeavor to foster a cat. We included all of the steps from the app to bringing the animal home and highlighted the information and resources provided by the organizaiton in order to facilitate a smooth and pleasant experience. We developed our flow for all three branches and in turn, a prototype with no dead ends.

Feature Prioritization

After conducting a multitude of interviews and surveys, our team sat down together to analyze the responses and prioritize the potential features. We measured the consistency of responses from our individual interviews, surveys, and our conversations with our stakeholder. From there, we brainstormed using a "what if" scenario and a "how might we?" prompt to come up with creative solutions. Finally, we charted our ideas and prioritized the most exciting and doable features that would make the most impact on the user and the organization. Our Priorities were the pet match swipe feature, the convenient appointment scheduling, and the ability to schedule a call through the app.
